Fit and Propensity Model
Fit (x-axis) is structural: house size, location, org structure, concessions potential, and any account relationship to an existing OTS customer.

Propensity (y-axis) is timing: growing seasons, new stages, capital campaigns, hiring, and signs of frustration with their current tools. Together they place a venue in one of four zones:
Priority now
High fit, high propensity. Reach out.
Opportunistic
Low fit, high propensity. Worth a lighter touch.
Nurture
High fit, low propensity. Good account, wrong time.
Deprioritize
Low fit, low propensity. Not worth the effort yet.
Fit: structural, 5 × 0–20
House & Venue Size0–20
How well seating capacity and scale match OTS's sub-enterprise ideal customer profile. Best fit is small-to-mid: not arena-scale, not a single-room space with negligible capacity.
Location & Market0–20
How favorable the market and location context looks for OTS: a region with performing arts density, with no indication of being an outlier location that complicates service.
Org Structure & Scale Fit0–20
Nonprofit, school, or community structure at sub-enterprise scale that does not require enterprise-grade donor/CRM tooling. Scored low if the org clearly needs enterprise donation management.
Concessions & Merch Potential0–20
Evidence the venue sells or could sell concessions or merchandise alongside tickets, the area where OTS beats consumer tools like Eventbrite.
Account Relationship0–20
Evidence of a parent organization, sibling venue, or affiliated entity that is already an OTS customer. Scored 10 (neutral) when no such relationship is found.

Propensity: timing, 5 × 0–20
Season & Show Growth0–20
Evidence of an increasing number of shows or an expanding season year over year.
Stage / Venue Expansion0–20
Evidence of a new or additional stage, second performance space, or physical expansion.
Capital Campaign & Press Momentum0–20
Evidence of a capital campaign, renovation, groundbreaking, or notable positive press coverage suggesting momentum.
Hiring Signals0–20
Evidence of relevant hiring (box office, patron services, general manager, marketing) suggesting growth.
Tooling Pain / Switching Signal0–20
Evidence of frustration with their current platform, a recent or rumored platform switch, or public complaints about ticketing/box office limitations.

Each category is scored by the model against these definitions, using public web search as its only source. A category defaults to 10 (neutral) when there isn't enough public evidence to score it confidently either way, rather than guessing toward an extreme. The two axis totals (each 0–100) are then summed in code from these ten scores, not assigned as a single holistic number, so every point on the quadrant traces back to a specific, visible reason. Struck-through items above are documented for the roadmap but are not collected or scored yet.
